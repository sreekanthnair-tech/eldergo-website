Our Story

We started ElderGo because we couldn't find anyone we'd trust with our own parents. So we built one. And we hold ourselves to that same standard — for every single traveller.

The Personal Obsession

I've always been a bit obsessive about travel planning. Not the "pick a package and go" kind — the kind where you spend weeks researching so that every day of the trip feels right. The right pace. The right hotel. The right restaurant for lunch on Day 3.

Over the years, I planned itineraries for friends who didn't have the time or patience to do it themselves. They'd follow the plan, come back happy, and ask me to make the next one.

The Problem

Then my parents retired. They wanted to see the world. They'd spent decades working, raising a family, putting everyone else first — and now, finally, they had the time.

But international travel at 60+ is complicated. Visas, airports, unfamiliar food, medical concerns, the fear of being a burden. They needed someone to handle everything. I looked for a tour operator who could take them. Someone who'd plan the trip the way I would — thoughtful pacing, good hotels, real experiences, no rushing, no cutting corners.

I couldn't find one. Everything was either budget packages designed for 25-year-olds, or overpriced luxury tours that treated seniors like they were fragile.

The First Trip

So I did what I always do — I planned it myself. I took my parents on their first group trip. I handled everything. The itinerary, the hotels, the meals, the sightseeing, the transfers. I made sure they never had to think about logistics for a single moment.

They came back and wouldn't stop talking about it. For weeks. The photos went to every relative. Every neighbour heard the stories. And then something happened that I didn't expect — my parents' friends wanted to come next time.

The Accidental Company

The next trip had six seniors. The one after that had twelve. I was still working at the time, squeezing trip planning into evenings and weekends. But the demand kept growing — not because of marketing, but because every group came back and told their friends.

At some point, I realised this wasn't a side project anymore. These weren't my parents' friends asking for a favour. These were families trusting me with their loved ones. That trust deserved a real company, real accountability, and a team that shared the same obsession with getting every detail right.

That's how ElderGo was born. Not from a business plan. From a waiting list.

— Sreekanth Nair, Founder

The Journey So Far

2018

First trip. Two travellers - our own parents.

2019

Parents' friends wanted in. First group trip 6 seniors. Then 12. Then a waiting list.

2020-2022

COVID brought travel to a halt. But the demand didn't disappear it waited. Families stayed in touch, asking "when can we go again?"

2023

Travel resumes. Trips restart. The waiting list was still there.

2024

ElderGo formally registered as Eldaura Senior Adventures Pvt Ltd. A real company to match the real responsibility.

2025

First full year. 220+ seniors. 13 group tours. 7 countries - Bhutan, Vietnam, Bali, Sri Lanka, Dubai, Kenya, Japan. Rated 4.96 on Google.

2026

Scaling to 4 departures per month across international, domestic, pilgrimage, and weekend getaways.

Process

How Every Trip Gets Made

1

Research

We spend weeks researching every new destination. Hotels are shortlisted, visited, and vetted in person before they make our list. Routes are driven. Restaurants are tested. We don't sell an itinerary we haven't experienced.

2

Design

Every day is planned for seniors - comfortable pacing, rest breaks built in, no early morning rushes. Each itinerary goes through multiple revisions before it's approved.

3

Prepare

Our tour managers are briefed on every traveller - dietary needs, medical considerations, mobility levels. We prepare for what we hope never happens.

4

Execute

One dedicated tour manager stays with the group from Day 1 to the last day. Not a local guide at each stop. One person who knows your name, your needs, and your preferences.

5

Improve

After every trip, we debrief. What worked, what didn't, what can be better. Our itineraries evolve with every single departure.

Operating Principles

What We Believe

WE VET EVERYTHING PERSONALLY.

Every hotel on our list has been visited and inspected - by our founders or our team. We check the rooms, the food, the accessibility, the service. If it doesn't meet our standard, it doesn't make the cut. No exceptions.

THE ITINERARY IS THE PRODUCT.

We spend more time designing each journey than most operators spend on their entire catalogue. The difference between a good trip and an unforgettable one is in the details most operators skip.

WE NEVER COMPROMISE ON QUALITY TO HIT A PRICE.

If a 4-star hotel is what the experience needs, that's what we book - even if it means our price is slightly higher than a competitor's. Our travellers don't come to us for the cheapest option. They come for the best one.

The People Who Travel With You

Your tour manager isn't a guide who shows up at tourist spots. They're a single, dedicated person who stays with your group from the moment you board the bus to the moment you're safely back home.

How We Select Them

Patience first, experience second. We look for people who genuinely enjoy spending time with seniors not just people with tourism degrees.

How We Train Them

Every tour manager is first-aid trained and briefed on emergency response protocols. They shadow experienced managers on multiple trips before leading a group themselves.

How We Improve

After every tour, managers submit detailed feedback what worked, what didn't, what can be improved. This feedback directly shapes the next departure.
